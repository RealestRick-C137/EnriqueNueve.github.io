1. Intro

  A key task in machine learning is density estimation. Density estimation is the process of estimating from a set of samples the underlying distribution of the data. Density estimation can provide insight into the probability of a sample but also can be used in more direct applications such as classification, denoising, missing input imputation, and data synthesis. In this post, a density estimation method by the name of MADE (Masked Autoencoder for Density Estimation) is examined along with a code implementation in Tf2.

2. Autoregressive Property

  MADE is built around the chain rule of probabity. The chain rule of probability states that a joint probability distribution can be expressed by an expansion of its conditional distributions in the following form expressed by the equation below.

  If an autoencoder was able to output the individual pieces of an expanded joint probability distribution in the form of the chain rule of probability, optimizing cross-entropy, assuming the input space is over binary values, leads to minimizing the negative log-likelihood. Thus, allowing an autoencoder to perform density estimation. MADE does just that, learns the individual components of an expanded joint probability distribution in the form of the chain rule of probability. This is possible by applying a series of masks to the weight layers of the autoencoder such that the connections properly follow the conditional distribution constraints of the chain rule of probability.
